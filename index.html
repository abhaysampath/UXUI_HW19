Farm Animal Rescue Of Maine

Case Study: Nonprofit Website Redesign

About the Project

The goal of this project was to re-design a non-profit website with the user experience in mind. Our team selected Farm Animal Rescue of Maine after noticing that they had a lot of information for display, but had organized it all into single long pages.

We decided to reorganize the website to divide the posts into smaller pages accessible through menus. This helped users that knew where they wanted to go navigate to their destination quickly, while also giving users that wanted to browse the non-profit's offerings an informative landing page to start on and start exploring from.

Tasks and Challenges

Website needs an aesthetic design and a call to action. It doesn't provide users with enough information to plan their trip, which is causing them to choose other destinations

Tools Used

Adobe Xd: Create prototype

Google Forms: Create Survey, Collect Data

Miro: Brainstorming, Gathering, Sorting Data

Figma: Shareable space to collect ideas

Research Methodology

Conducted phone and online surveys to gather data about why people visit farms and what data they are looking for on the farmâ€™s website. Analyzed 4 other farm websites to find strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.

Compiled survey results to find specific areas for improvement. Created a User Persona with specific behavioral demographics, needs, goal, and pain points.

Used the persona we created to create a user journey and drew storyboards. Reorganized the website sitemap to organize information into menus and submenus.

User Experience Storyboard

Wireframing & Prototyping

Using the updated sitemap, we created lo-fi wireframes, then iterated the design with further testing and developed a clickable high-fidelity prototype starting with the site navigation.

The prototypes focused on two main tasks:

1) Registering for the farm camp

2) Donating to the animal rescue nonprofit.
